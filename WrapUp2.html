Red Bull Racing History

Since its inception in 2005, Red Bull Racing has grown from a disruptive force into one of F1's most successful teams, with 122 wins, eight Drivers' titles, and six Constructors' titles. Led by Christian Horner and designer Adrian Newey, the team secured its first win in 2009, followed by four consecutive double titles with Sebastian Vettel from 2010 to 2013. The hybrid era brought a fallow period, but a new generation emerged with Daniel Ricciardo, and then Max Verstappen arrived in 2016, stunning the paddock by winning his first race for the team at the Spanish Grand Prix. Wins remained sporadic until Honda power arrived in 2019, leading to Max's first world title in a dramatic 2021 season finale. New regulations in 2022 saw the RB18 dominate, and with Sergio Pérez alongside, Red Bull reclaimed the Constructors' title after nine years. The RB19 then made history as the most successful season ever, delivering a sixth Constructors' title and Max's third straight Drivers' championship. Max carried that form into 2024, winning seven of the first ten races, and despite rivals closing the gap, he claimed a fourth straight Drivers' title in Las Vegas, though the team narrowly missed out on the Constructors' title. As of 2026 many of the key figures in the team have either been fired or quit for other teams.
